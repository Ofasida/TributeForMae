<div id="tribute-info">
  <h2 id="tribute-info-header">Who is Dr. Mae C. Jemison?</h2>

<h3 id="Early-headers">Early Life</h3>
<p>Born on October 17, 1956, in Decatur, Alabama, Mae Jemison is the youngest child of Charlie and Dorothy Jemison.<br><br>
At age three, her family relocated to Chicago, Illinois, seeking better educational opportunities.<br><br>
Growing up she was always on the honor roll and graduated in 1973 when she was only 16 years old from high school.</p>
<h3 id="EDU-headers">Education</h3>
<ul> 
<li>She attended Stanford University, and graduated in 1977 with a Bachelor of Science degree in chemical engineering and a Bachelor of Arts degree in African American studies.</li>
<li>After graduating from Stanford University, Jemison continued her education and attended Cornell University Medical College where she earned her Doctor of Medicine degree in 1981.</li>
<li>After graduating from medical school, she interned at the Los Angeles County Medical Center and later practiced general medicine.</li>
  </ul>
  
<h2 id="tribute-career">
    Career Journey
   </h2>
<h3 id="Meds-headers">Medical Career</h3>
 <ul>
  <li>Dr. Jemison interned at Los Angeles County-University of Southern California Medical Center and worked as a general practitioner.</li>
  <li>Dr. Jemison knew she want to work on Biomedical Research and work in developing countries, so she took her expertise to the Peace Corps and became a medical officer in Sierra Leone and Liberia from 1983 to 1985.</li>
<li>In her experience as a Peace Corps Volunter she learned many valuable skills. In an interview she states,"On call twenty four hours a day, seven days a week for two and a half years, in a place that could be so unforgiving of mistakes, I gained flexibility, knowledge, interpersonal relationship skills, and an appreciation of the challenges life poses to so many people on this planet â€¦ The insights I gained into the misconceptions of lifestyles and the challenges and opportunities in poor countries nourished my imagination. I understood. What we decide to do with our talents is up to each one of us and is based on our view of life and our desires."</li>
   <li>After returning home from the Peace Corps, she went to work as a general practitioner with CIGNA Health Plans of California.</li>
   </ul>
  <p> All her accomplishments so far were great to her, but she still craved more. Dr. Jemison wanted to learn more, and she always had a fascination with Space Exploration.</p>
 

<!-- The accomplishments that Dr. Jemison had in her career. -->
  <div>
  <h3 id="NASA-headers">NASA Career</h3>
   <p> While at CIGNA, Dr Jemison began taking graduate engineering classes and applied to NASA in October 1985 for admission to the astronaut program.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Following the Challenger disaster in January 1986, the selection process was delayed, but she reapplied and was selected in June 1987, she was accepted as one of fifteen astronaut candidates becoming the first Black woman admitted to the program.</li>
    <li> On September 12, 1992, At 36 years old, Dr.Jemison flew into space aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavour on mission STS-47, making her the first African American woman to travel into space.</li>
   <li>She completed her training in 1988, and served in 1992 as a mission specialist on the space shuttle Endeavour.</li>
    <li>During the shuttle mission she conducted experiments in life sciences, material sciences, and was co-investigator in the bone cell research experiment.</li>
 </ul>
